High(er) voltage and transistors Project Overview

Introduction

For Assignment 5, I built a circuit that controls a high-power DC fan using an N-MOSFET transistor and an IR remote. The circuit separates logic power (Arduino) from motor power (external 12V supply). The fan operates in two states: ON and OFF, controlled wirelessly via an IR sensor using the IRremote library.

Hardware

The hardware setup includes:

  • Arduino Uno
  • 12V DC Fan
  • N-MOSFET Transistor
  • IR Receiver Module
  • IR Remote Control
  • Flyback Diode (1N4007)
  • External 12V Power Supply (2A)
  • A breadboard and jumper wires for connections

Schematic

Circuit

  • N-MOSFET (DMT6009LCT): Acts as a switch to turn the fan ON or OFF based on the signal from the Arduino.
  • 12V DC Fan
  • Flyback Diode (1N4007): Protects the MOSFET from voltage spikes when the fan turns OFF by safely redirecting excess energy.
  • IR Receiver (TSOP382 or similar): Receives infrared signals from the remote and sends them to the Arduino for processing.
  • 12V Power Supply (2A): Powers the fan, ensuring it operates at full speed without overloading the Arduino.
  • IR Remote Control: Sends commands to the IR receiver to turn the fan ON or OFF wirelessly.
